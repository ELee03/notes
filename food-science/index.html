Food Science

Applied Chemistry · Biochemistry · Thermodynamics · Sensory Science

Cooking is applied science. Every time you sear a steak, bake bread, or make a sauce, you are running chemical reactions — controlling temperature, pH, water activity, and protein structure with the goal of producing a specific result. Most cooks do this by feel, by rule of thumb, by tradition. Food science does it by understanding the underlying mechanisms.

This cluster covers the science of what happens to food when you cook it. Not nutritional science. Not food safety regulations. The actual chemistry and physics: why does bread rise, why does meat sear, why does fat emulsify with water, why does fermentation produce flavour. McGee's On Food and Cooking is the primary reference — one of the rare books that is simultaneously readable and scientifically rigorous.

Prerequisites: None strictly required. Basic STEM literacy helps (atoms, molecules, what temperature means) but is not assumed. Chemistry and biochemistry concepts are introduced inline as needed. This cluster is designed to be readable by anyone curious about why cooking works.
