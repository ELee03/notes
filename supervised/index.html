Supervised Learning
Regression · Classification · Generalisation · Neural Networks · Backpropagation · Optimisation
Supervised learning is the foundation of modern machine learning: given examples of inputs paired with correct outputs, learn a function that generalises to unseen inputs. It underlies spam filters, medical diagnosis, speech recognition, image classifiers, and the neural networks that power large language models.
This cluster builds the subject from first principles. It begins with the formal framework — hypothesis spaces, empirical risk, and the generalisation problem — then covers the classical algorithms (linear regression, logistic regression, SVMs, decision trees, ensembles) before developing the full neural network story: forward pass, backpropagation, and the practical craft of training. The primary references are Bishop & Bishop (2024) and Goodfellow, Bengio & Courville (2016).
