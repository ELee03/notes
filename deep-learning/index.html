Deep Learning
Residual Networks · CNNs · RNNs · LSTMs · Attention · Transformers · BERT & GPT
Deep learning is the study of neural networks with many layers — and the architectural innovations that make those networks trainable, expressive, and general. This cluster begins where the Supervised Learning cluster ends: with the basic neural network and backpropagation. It then introduces the key architectural ideas — residual connections, convolution, recurrence, and attention — that underpin every major model in use today. The culminating lesson is the Transformer, the architecture behind GPT, BERT, LLaMA, and virtually every large language model in existence.
Prerequisites: Supervised Learning L8–L11 (neural networks, backpropagation, training, probabilistic perspectives). In particular: the forward pass, gradient computation, softmax, cross-entropy, and Adam are assumed.
