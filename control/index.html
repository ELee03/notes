Control Theory
Feedback · Stability · Frequency Domain · State Space · Optimal Control
How do you make a system do what you want — reliably, even when it is disturbed, even when your model is imperfect? Control theory gives you the language and the tools to answer that question. From a thermostat to an aircraft autopilot to a robot arm, the same core ideas recur: feedback, stability, and the tradeoff between performance and robustness.
This cluster builds control theory from first principles. It covers classical frequency-domain methods (Bode, Nyquist, root locus, PID) and the modern state-space approach (controllability, observability, LQR, and observers), ending with the separation principle that lets you design state feedback and state estimation independently. The primary references are Franklin, Powell & Emami-Naeini and Ogata, supplemented by Broucke's ECE356/557 course notes.
