Control Theory
Feedback Systems · Frequency Domain · State Space
How to make systems do what you want. Control theory gives you the language to reason about stability, performance, and robustness — from a thermostat to a spacecraft attitude controller.

References
These notes draw on several sources. Listed for context, not as prerequisites.
Textbooks
  • Ogata — Modern Control Engineering (5th ed.)
    The standard undergraduate reference. Dense but comprehensive.
  • Franklin, Powell & Emami-Naeini — Feedback Control of Dynamic Systems (8th ed.)
    Strong on physical intuition and worked examples.
  • Dorf & Bishop — Modern Control Systems (13th ed.)
    Broad coverage; useful for quick reference.
Related courses
ECE356 (Systems Control) and ECE557 (Advanced Topics) at the University of Toronto cover this material across two terms. Some course notes from those offerings are referenced where relevant.
